Reinventing the Guerlain Experience 

Client
Guerlain customers

Role
UX/UI & Motion Design

Year
2025

For this project, I chose to dive into the heart of Guerlain’s universe — approaching design not merely as a methodology, but as a sensitive exploration of customer experience. My goal was to understand what truly moves visitors of such an iconic House and to translate that emotion into an elegant, intuitive digital journey.

I began by using the serious game “The Public Service Design Game” to frame the challenge and identify major friction points. Then, I conducted a guerrilla test in front of the Champs-Élysées boutique, observing and interviewing passersby. This fieldwork helped me create an empathy map and identify key areas for improvement.

During the ideation phase, I used the “Crazy Eight” and “How Might We” methods to quickly generate a wide range of ideas. These concepts were then prioritized and formalized in an idea book before being prototyped on Figma. I designed a refreshed design system, more aligned with Guerlain’s identity, enriched with micro interactions across mobile and desktop to enhance fluidity and immersion. Prototypes were tested and iterated using an Atomic Research approach, improving both readability and personalized navigation.

Finally, I incorporated accessibility (RGAA) and eco-design principles in line with Guerlain’s ethical commitments. This project allowed me to combine user research, creativity, and technical rigor to craft a refined digital experience — true to the demanding codes of the luxury world.
